# Temporary Parking Decision

Date: 2026-05-31

We are temporarily parking the browser walkthrough video product after MVP0.

## Reasons

1. The product is heavier than a first cash-flow project should be.
   Browser automation, model routing, visual QA, rendering, payments, retries, and multilingual variants create a lot of moving parts before revenue is proven.

2. Acquisition is still uncertain.
   The strongest wedge is promising, but it is not yet obvious that founders will search for it, understand it quickly, and pay without education or calls.

3. Free usage can become expensive.
   Agentic browser runs and video renders are easy to abuse or waste. A useful free preview would need strict gating, otherwise failed runs can burn time and API/render budget.

4. Quality risk is high.
   A walkthrough video is only valuable if it is visibly correct: right page, right language, smooth cursor, no cookie banner, no broken PDF scroll, no blurry output. Each failure hurts trust.

5. The real moat is workflow reliability, not the model.
   The durable value would be in Playwright repair logic, frame QA, render templates, multilingual variants, and cost controls. That is valuable, but not tiny.

6. AI platform capability may move fast.
   Official agent/browser/video tooling from major AI providers could reduce the implementation burden later. Waiting may make the product cheaper to build.

7. Simpler near-term ROI options are available.
   For a first money-focused project, a simpler wedge with lower infra cost, less QA risk, and clearer distribution may be a better use of time.

## What We Keep

- MVP0 code for upload-based polished MP4 previews.
- The related browser tutorial workflow as proof that agentic walkthrough production is possible.
- Product insight: the stronger opportunity is likely "written browser task to polished walkthrough video", not just "raw MP4 polish".

## Reopen Criteria

Revisit this when at least one is true:

- API/browser-agent costs drop enough that storyboard and QA can run cheaply.
- Official tools make browser-to-video workflows much easier.
- A clear distribution channel appears.
- A buyer segment repeatedly asks for multilingual/tutorial walkthrough videos.
- A paid manual/concierge version gets demand first.
